Professional Identity

The Transpersonal Therapist

Skills, ethics, and presence for holding transformative therapeutic space

Qualities of the Transpersonal Therapist

The transpersonal therapist is more than a clinician—they are a guide, witness, and container for profound transformation. This work demands technical excellence, ethical clarity, and deep personal development.

Our training cultivates these essential qualities through supervised practice, personal therapy, and ongoing mentorship in working with holotropic states of consciousness.

Core Competencies

Safety & Ethics

Creating safe containers, maintaining boundaries, recognizing contraindications, and upholding EUROTAS ethical standards in all work.

Somatic Awareness

Reading body language, tracking energy, facilitating focused bodywork, and integrating trauma-informed practices.

Navigating Non-Ordinary States

Guiding breathwork journeys, supporting spiritual emergence, and honoring the inner healer without imposing interpretation.

Integration Skills

Helping clients make meaning of experiences, grounding insights into daily life, and supporting long-term transformation.

Personal Development Requirements

Own Therapy Process

Complete your own breathwork and transpersonal therapy to understand the journey from inside and work through personal shadow material.

Supervised Practice

Work with client models and real cases under expert supervision, receiving feedback on technique, presence, and ethical decision-making.

Peer Learning

Exchange sessions with fellow trainees, practice giving and receiving in the client/therapist dyad, and build community support.

Ongoing Study

Continue education in trauma, psychopharmacology, cultural competency, and emerging research in consciousness and psychedelics.

Begin Your Path

Our training programs provide the structure, support, and mentorship to develop as a skilled transpersonal therapist.
