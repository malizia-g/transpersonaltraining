What is Transpersonal Psychology?

Transpersonal psychology integrates the spiritual and transcendent aspects of the human experience with the framework of modern psychology. It goes beyond the personal ego to explore states of consciousness that connect us to something greater than ourselves.

Founded by pioneers like Abraham Maslow, Stanislav Grof, and Ken Wilber, transpersonal therapy recognizes that healing often involves accessing non-ordinary states of consciousness—through breathwork, meditation, psychedelics, or spontaneous spiritual emergence.

Our approach combines clinical rigor with openness to mystical and transformative experiences, creating a container where the soul's journey is honored alongside symptom relief.

Key Concepts

Perinatal Matrices (Grof)

Stan Grof's framework of birth trauma and its impact on psychological development. The four Basic Perinatal Matrices (BPM) offer a map for understanding deep unconscious material.

The Spectrum of Consciousness (Wilber)

Ken Wilber's integral model spanning pre-personal, personal, and transpersonal stages of development, emphasizing the "pre/trans fallacy."

The Farther Reaches (Maslow)

Abraham Maslow's work on peak experiences, self-actualization, and the need for meaning beyond material success.

Psychedelic-Assisted Therapy

The renaissance of research into psilocybin, MDMA, and LSD as catalysts for profound healing when used in therapeutic contexts.
