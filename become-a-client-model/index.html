Free Counselling and Supervised Practice

Joining as a client model gives you access to free counselling or free psychotherapy sessions while helping our trainees develop practical counselling skills in a safe and supervised setting.

Sessions focus on real life themes such as repeating cycles, behavioural patterns, emotional blocks, and personal growth goals. All work is supervised by experienced teachers.

Depending on availability, client models may also be invited to selected breathwork seminars conducted by therapists in training under faculty supervision.

What to Look for in a Transpersonal Psychotherapist

Finding the right transpersonal psychotherapist matters; perhaps more than in some other modalities, because the work can touch on the most vulnerable parts of who you are. Whether you arrived here through your own research, a friend's recommendation, or a growing sense that something was missing from traditional therapies, the following aspects are worth considering. A good transpersonal therapist embodies the principles of transpersonal therapy not just in sessions but in their own ongoing commitment to growth and self-examination.

  1. Verified training and qualifications: Look for someone who has completed a recognized transpersonal psychology degree, an accredited transpersonal psychology school, or equivalent psychotherapy training. Accreditation from a professional body — such as EUROTAS, ensures observance to educational and ethical standards. Be cautious of people who claim transpersonal expertise without verifiable credentials.
  2. A solid clinical foundation: The best transpersonal therapists do not float above the ground. They combine spiritual awareness with proper clinical skill; the ability to assess risk, work safely with trauma, and recognize when a referral is needed. Their integrative therapy education should be evident in their capacity to meet you wherever you are, not just where the transpersonal techniques are most interesting.
  3. Their own personal practice: A transpersonal therapist should have an ongoing contemplative or spiritual practice; meditation, breathwork, personal therapy, supervision, or some combination. This is not optional. It is what gives the therapist the depth and presence to hold space for your process. Do not be shy to ask.
  4. Ethical clarity and professional boundaries: A responsible transpersonal psychotherapist maintains clear boundaries, works within an ethical code, and is transparent about their methods and limitations. They should never push spiritual beliefs onto you or use their position to manipulate. Transpersonal therapy at its best is empowering, never dogmatic.
  5. Cultural humility and inclusivity: The field draws on many spiritual and cultural traditions. The second wave has placed increasing emphasis on diversity and social context (Hartelius et al., 2021). Your therapist should approach this material with genuine respect and openness to your own background, rather than imposing a particular worldview.
  6. A collaborative stance: The transpersonal therapeutic relationship is, at its best, a genuine partnership. Your therapist should welcome questions, respect your pace, and adjust based on your feedback. The best transpersonal psychotherapy education emphasizes this collaborative, client-centered ethic from the very beginning of training.
